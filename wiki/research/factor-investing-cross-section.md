# Factor investing & the cross-section

**What it is.** The literature underpinning the project's pivot to cross-sectional
ranking. (Reference stub — add specific papers as we cite them.)

**Key takeaways (as used here).**
- **Fundamental Law of Active Management** (Grinold & Kahn): **IR ≈ IC · √(breadth)**
  — the identity driving [[information-coefficient-and-ir]] and the whole
  [[breadth]] argument.
- **Cross-sectional momentum** (Jegadeesh–Titman): rank assets by past return, long
  winners / short losers. The `mom_12_1 / 6_1 / 3_1` features in
  [[exp-003-xsection-fx]] are exactly this.
- **Value & reversal** — long-term mean-reversion ("value") and short-term reversal
  as complementary factors; both are features in exp-003.
- **Baseline discipline** — any ML ranker must be compared to these *simple* factor
  sorts; if it doesn't beat a momentum sort, it adds nothing (see exp-003's key
  question).
